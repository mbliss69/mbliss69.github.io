Whistler Blackcomb Ski Resort

Blackcomb: This is the go-to mountain for many skiers and riders, with its supreme upper alpine area, massive glade options and wide open powder bowls. If you’re seeking the gnarliest run, hike up past the Glacier T-Bar along the ridge known as Spanky’s Ladder to drop into Ruby Bowl for an adrenaline blasting double-black diamond shred. For a less intense yet still thrilling bowl, hike up to the Blackcomb Glacier for plenty of fresh tracks all day. Ziggy’s Meadow is usually the spot for the best powder.

Whistler: This is also the go-to mountain for another group of many skiers and riders up the Whistler Village Gondola or Creekside godnola. Much of Whistler Mountain feels like backcountry riding while you’re still safely in bounds, especially on a bluebird powder day. Head up to Symphony Bowl for some outstanding runs that the whole family can enjoy. Franz’s Run is another premium spot with amazing groomed runs if you get there early (which you can do with the Earlybird). The best moguls around are on Grand Finale.
